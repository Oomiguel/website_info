Galaxies
Galaxies are massive collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter that are held together by gravity. There are billions of galaxies in the observable universe, each with its own unique shape, size, and characteristics. Galaxies come in different types, including spiral, elliptical, and irregular, and they can range in size from dwarf galaxies with only a few million stars to massive galaxies with trillions of stars. These majestic structures are the building blocks of the universe, and they hold clues to the evolution of the cosmos, including the formation of stars, galaxies, and even life itself.
