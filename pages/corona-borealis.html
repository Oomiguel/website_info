Corona Borealis

1.General Information

  • Corona Borealis is a small constellation in the Northern Celestial Hemisphere and is one of the 48 constellations listed by Ptolemy. Its Latin name, inspired by its shape, means "northern crown". In classical mythology, it represented the crown given by the god Dionysus to the Cretan princess Ariadne and set by her in the heavens. The brightest star is the magnitude 2.2 Alpha Coronae Borealis. R Coronae Borealis is the prototype of a rare class of giant stars—the R Coronae Borealis variables—which are extremely hydrogen deficient.

  • T Coronae Borealis, also known as the Blaze Star, is another unusual type of variable star known as a recurrent nova. ADS 9731 and Sigma Coronae Borealis are multiple star systems with six and five components respectively. Abell 2065 is a highly concentrated galaxy cluster one billion light-years from the Solar System containing more than 400 members, and is itself part of the larger Corona Borealis Supercluster. 

    2.Stars

  • Coronae Borealis is a constellation with seven stars, except for Alpha Coronae Borealis, which is the brightest of them. Johann Bayer gave twenty stars in Corona Borealis Bayer designations from Alpha to Upsilon in his 1603 star atlas Uranometria. Zeta Coronae Borealis was noted to be a double star by later astronomers and its components named Zeta1 and Zeta2. John Flamsteed classed Nu Coronae Borealis as a single star and named it 20 and 21 Coronae Borealis in his catalogue. Chinese astronomers deemed nine stars to make up the asterism, adding Pi and Rho Coronae Borealis.

  • Corona Borealis is home to two remarkable variable stars: T Coronae Borealis is a cataclysmic variable star, also known as the Blaze Star, and R Coronae Borealis is a yellow-hued variable supergiant star, over 7000 light-years from Earth. Coronae Borealis is a red giant that shines with a luminosity 16,643 times that of the Sun and has a surface temperature of 3033 K. It ranges between magnitudes 5.8 and 14.1 over a period of 360 days, with V Coronae Borealis being one of the reddest stars in the sky. W Coronae Borealis ranges between magnitudes 7.8 and 14.3 over a period of 238 days, and RR Coronae Borealis is a M3-type semiregular variable star that ranges between magnitudes 7.3 and 8.2 over 60.8 days. 

    3.Mythology

  • Corona Borealis is a constellation that was linked to the legend of Theseus and the minotaur. It was generally considered to represent a crown given by Dionysus to Ariadne, the daughter of Minos of Crete, after she had been abandoned by the Athenian prince Theseus. In Welsh mythology, it was called Caer Arianrhod, "the Castle of the Silver Circle", and was the heavenly abode of the Lady Arianrhod. To the ancient Balts, it was known as Darelis, the "flower garden". The Arabs called it Alphecca, which means "separated" or "broken up".

  • The Wailwun people of northwestern New South Wales saw it as mullion wollai "eagle's nest", with Altair and Vega (each called mullion) accompanying it. The Wardaman people of northern Australia held it to be a gathering point for Men's Law, Women's Law and Law of both sexes come together and consider matters of existence.
